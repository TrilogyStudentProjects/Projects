Crude Oil Production & GDP Impact on Global Warming

Objective

The project’s primary purpose is to understand if such factors as crude oil production and GDP growth impact global temperature, causing Arctic and Antarctic sea ice to melt.

Problem

Global warming is the unusually rapid increase in Earth’s average surface temperature over the past century primarily due to the greenhouse gases released by people burning fossil fuels. The term is frequently used interchangeably with the term climate change, though the latter refers to both human and naturally produced warming and their effects on our planet. The average increase in Earth’s global surface temperature is most commonly measured.

Some key indicators of climate change include:

  • Increases in global land and ocean temperature
  • Rising sea levels
  • Ice loss at the Earth’s poles and in mountain glaciers
  • Extreme weather frequency and severity changes
  • Hurricanes
  • Heat waves
  • Wildfires
  • Droughts
  • Floods
  • Floods
  • Precipitation

This project looks into the connection between rising temperatures and crude oil production.

Process

Data upload

Used following Pandas methods to read the data from various data sources:

  1. read_excel
  2. read_csv
  3. read_htm

Types of Plot

  1. Used plot() to plot some data sets.
  2. Used hvplot() to plot graphs for data sets.
  3. Used heat map to plot the correlations.
  4. Used Altair to plot map and graph.

New packages

  1. Imported Altair, math, and vega_datasets for this project.

Data cleaning and analysis

  1. Used Transpose (T) method to convert rows to columns.
  2. Used column rename function.
  3. Used drop function to drop columns.
  4. Used pct_change(fill_method='ffill') function while calculating percentage change.
  5. Used dropna function to drop null.
  6. Used load_dotenv() and getenv() to access API.
  7. Used Monte Carlo’s simulation to predict a temperature rise for the next ten years.
  8. Used Monte Carlo’s simulation to predict glacier loss for the next ten years (both Arctic and Antarctic).
  9. Used Concat() to combine various data sets like world GDP, crude oil production, world temperature, Arctic glacier data, and Antarctic glacier data.
  10. Used Corr() function to calculate the correlation between various data frames.
