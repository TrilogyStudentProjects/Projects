Tokenization of Private Equity

Objective

The project’s primary purpose is to create a platform that allows any investor to participate in private equity funds that are generally illiquid and offered to corporations and the ultra-wealthy. We will raise funds through a crowd sale and then trade in the secondary market with a lock-in period of 10 years. Once the money is raised, the fund will then seed startups across a variety of industries in order to mimic S&P 500 performance.

Tokenization is the process of issuing a blockchain token (also known as a security token) that digitally represents a real tradable asset. These security tokens are created through a security token offering (STO).

A security token offering (STO)/tokenized IPO is a type of public offering in which tokenized digital securities, known as security tokens, are sold in cryptocurrency exchanges. Tokens can be used to trade real financial assets such as equities and fixed income, and use a blockchain virtual ledger system to store and validate token transactions.

  • Private equity funds can now raise funds through the creation of their own initial coin/security token offering
  • After the funds are raised, a market for investing is created for initial investors to sell and buy their coins
  • The expected fund returns are generated using statistical techniques such as the Autoregressive Moving Average (ARMA) and Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA).

Problem

Our token will offer the potential for a more efficient and fair financial market by allowing investors access to financial investment through digital currency, increasing accessibility into a market that is traditionally only offered to a select few.

Solution

Some key solutions that tokenization provides for both investors and sellers:

Liquidity: Tokens can be traded on secondary markets which will provide access to a broader base of traders and investors. This will increase liquidity in contrast to the traditional private equity fund structure.

Rapid settlement and cost-effective transactions: Token transactions are completed with smart contracts, and certain parts of the exchange process are automated. This automation will reduce administrative costs and allow for immutable same-day settlement.

Transparency: Tokens will have the token-holder’s rights and legal responsibilities embedded directly onto the token, along with an immutable record of ownership.

Compliance enforcement: Programs legal obligations into digital assets to ensure only eligible parties participate.

24/7 market: A marketplace that can run 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Crude Oil Production & GDP Impact on Global Warming

Objective

The project’s primary purpose is to understand if such factors as crude oil production and GDP growth impact global temperature, causing Arctic and Antarctic sea ice to melt.

Problem

Global warming is the unusually rapid increase in Earth’s average surface temperature over the past century primarily due to the greenhouse gases released by people burning fossil fuels. The term is frequently used interchangeably with the term climate change, though the latter refers to both human and naturally produced warming and their effects on our planet. The average increase in Earth’s global surface temperature is most commonly measured.

Some key indicators of climate change include:

  • Increases in global land and ocean temperature
  • Rising sea levels
  • Ice loss at the Earth’s poles and in mountain glaciers
  • Extreme weather frequency and severity changes
  • Hurricanes
  • Heat waves
  • Wildfires
  • Droughts
  • Floods
  • Floods
  • Precipitation

This project looks into the connection between rising temperatures and crude oil production.

Questions Posed for Analysis:

Based on the analysis performed, we were able to identify a correlation between crude oil production and temperature increase. The image below shows this correlation.

Our analysis found that there is not a significant correlation between GDP and temperature changes. A strong correlation could be possible, but with this analysis no significant correlation was found.

Nasdaq Predictive Model

Objective

Our project objective is to build something that will analyze live data and send us a daily list of stocks predicted to increase in value the following day. We want to identify stocks that can be expected to outperform the Nasdaq, and then create a way to send these alerts with the stock ticker symbols to alert for potential spikes in stock price.. Our project took two weeks to complete and we plan to continue to make improvements!

Problem

Have you ever sat down at 5 o’clock to watch “Mad Money” or other finance shows? As you listen to Jim Cramer speak very quickly, he lists off eight to ten companies that have been up “blank_%” and you think, “why haven’t I heard of these folks?” Well, if you’re anything like us, sometimes you miss one or two.

Solution

The idea is to help the average trader/investor who likes researching capital markets and active investing. But ultimately, we attempted to make something we would use ourselves.

The predictive random forest model was trained on all companies listed on the Nasdaq in the past year, with data beginning in April 2019 and ending in July 2020.

For a small fee, we were able to test over 900,000 data points; specifically looking at closing prices with regard to returns.

Contents of Repository

  1. handler: Contains the necessary libraries that are required for the model to operate in AWS infrastructure, as well as an older version of the model (handler.py) which runs on a local machine but not AWS.
  2. docker_deploy: Contains the most up-to-date model and necessary libraries that were executed on AWS Lambda (handler.zip), as well as the file necessary for deployment on AWS (Makefile).
  1. NASDAQ-historical-data: Contains historical data for the Nasdaq that the model is training and testing on, both as individual days and aggregated.
  2. Images: Images used in the repository.

Process of Creating the Model

As with all models, model quality relies on your data. We were able to find historical data for all Nasdaq tickers as of July 2020, with the ability to backtest for 10 years. We focused on YTD information so that we were able to factor in pandemic volatility.

It took some time to work with the dataset but, after we had a clean Pandas DataFrame, we were able to view each ticker’s “close” price. We were then able to calculate daily returns, including a shift to account for bias.

We chose Random Forest Classifier to accomplish this task for two reasons:

  1. There were plenty of data points for the model to learn.
  2. The data contained a couple of significant events such as:
  • Pre COVID
  • COVID Crash
  • COVID Recovery

After testing the model, we were able to predict returns with 100% accuracy, so naturally we believed something had to be wrong. We spent all of our time on AWS Sagemaker, a service provided by Amazon that allows you to prepare, build, train, and deploy machine learning models on the cloud.! Once we had the model trained and running without error, the team got together and uploaded the files through AWS S3 and passed our data into an AWS Lambda function. To finish, the “Makefile” had to run through CloudWatch SNS in order to make the daily or weekly email alert possible. This step provided us with the opportunity to receive feedback and connect with subscribers. Deploying the Model

For the model to be useful, it needed to be deployable. Again, we opted to use AWS to execute the model once a day. We ran into a potential issue with our file sizes, in particular the data and imported libraries. To solve this problem, we uploaded the data separately on S3, and then we used CloudWatch to configure the daily emails. After debugging, we were able to deploy and the email was requested.

  2. Improve email messaging to enhance user experience.
  3. Limit the number of tickers that our function returns to the top 15 companies.
